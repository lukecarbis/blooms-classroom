[PROLOGUE]
NARRATOR: Mr. Carbis adjusts his tie in the reflection of the classroom door. First day. First real class.
Mr. Carbis: I've studied every pedagogy textbook. I've rehearsed every lesson. I'm ready for this.
NARRATOR: He takes a deep breath and pushes open the door.
NARRATOR (classroom): Twelve eyes stare back at him. Some curious. Some indifferent. Some already looking at their phones.
Mr. Carbis (classroom): Good morning, everyone. I'm Mr. Carbis. Let's learn something together.
NARRATOR (classroom): The silence is deafening. A chair squeaks. Someone coughs.
Mr. Carbis (classroom): (Keep it simple. Start with what they know. Build from there.)


[LEVEL 1 INTRO — REMEMBER]
NARRATOR: LEVEL 1: REMEMBER
NARRATOR: Bloom's Taxonomy Level: Knowledge -- the foundation of all learning.
Mr. Carbis: Let's start with the basics. I need to see what they already know.
NARRATOR: Walk to students and press SPACE to ask recall questions.
NARRATOR: Keep the class average attention above the threshold before time runs out!
NARRATOR: Move: WASD or Arrow Keys | Interact: SPACE


[LEVEL 1 OUTRO]
NARRATOR: The bell rings. Students shuffle out, some actually smiling.
Mr. Carbis: That... that actually went well.
NARRATOR: He erases the whiteboard and looks at the empty room.
Mr. Carbis: They answered my questions. They remembered the material. This teaching thing might be straightforward after all.
NARRATOR: But as he packs his bag, a quiet thought nags at the edge of his mind...
Mr. Carbis: Did they really learn anything? Or did they just repeat what I said?


[LEVEL 2 INTRO — UNDERSTAND]
NARRATOR: LEVEL 2: UNDERSTAND
NARRATOR: Bloom's Taxonomy Level: Comprehension -- grasping meaning, not just facts.
Mr. Carbis: A student asked me 'but WHY does that matter?' yesterday. And I froze.
Mr. Carbis: They can parrot facts back, but I can see it in their eyes -- the understanding isn't there.
NARRATOR: New technique: Press E to THINK ALOUD -- model your thinking to nearby students.
NARRATOR: Some students are more easily distracted than others. Watch for the warning signs.


[LEVEL 2 OUTRO]
NARRATOR: A student lingers after class.
Student: Mr. Carbis? I think I actually get it now. Like, I can explain it to my friend.
NARRATOR: He watches the student leave, a warmth spreading in his chest.
Mr. Carbis: That's what it should feel like. Not just knowledge transferred, but understanding kindled.
Mr. Carbis: But understanding alone isn't enough either, is it? They need to DO something with it.


[LEVEL 3 INTRO — APPLY]
NARRATOR: LEVEL 3: APPLY
NARRATOR: Bloom's Taxonomy Level: Application -- using knowledge in new situations.
Mr. Carbis: I gave them a problem to solve yesterday. Open-ended. No right answer.
Mr. Carbis: Half of them stared at blank pages. They understood the concepts but couldn't USE them.
NARRATOR: New technique: Press Q near students to send them to LEARNING STATIONS.
NARRATOR: Students at stations restore attention on their own. Guide them to apply what they know!


[LEVEL 3 OUTRO]
NARRATOR: The classroom is scattered with half-finished worksheets and hastily drawn diagrams.
Mr. Carbis: It's messy. It's loud. It's... is this what learning looks like?
NARRATOR: He picks up one student's work. It's wrong in places, but the thinking behind it is remarkable.
Mr. Carbis: They're trying. Really trying. But are they thinking deeply enough?
NARRATOR: The honeymoon period is fading. The real challenge is just beginning.


[LEVEL 4 INTRO — ANALYZE]
NARRATOR: LEVEL 4: ANALYZE
NARRATOR: Bloom's Taxonomy Level: Analysis -- breaking apart ideas to understand their structure.
NARRATOR: Wednesday. Week six. Mr. Carbis sits in the staff room, staring at his coffee.
Mr. Carbis: A student told me my class was 'boring' today. Right to my face.
Mrs. Okafor: Stop trying to control their attention. Start giving them reasons to care.
Mr. Carbis: What if I asked questions instead of giving answers?
NARRATOR: Students now raise QUESTION BUBBLES. They disappear quickly -- get to them fast!
NARRATOR: Walk near a questioning student and press SPACE. Recall Prompt becomes SOCRATIC DIALOG -- a huge attention boost for the student and their neighbours.
NARRATOR: Press Q to start GROUP WORK -- gathering students at a desk to collaborate.


[LEVEL 4 OUTRO]
NARRATOR: Something shifts in the air.
Student: Wait -- so you're saying there ISN'T one right answer?
Mr. Carbis: I'm saying the question matters more than the answer.
NARRATOR: For the first time, Mr. Carbis sees genuine curiosity in their eyes.
NARRATOR: Not compliance. Not performance. Curiosity.
Mr. Carbis: Maybe this is what it's supposed to feel like.


[LEVEL 5 INTRO — SYNTHESIZE]
NARRATOR: LEVEL 5: SYNTHESIZE
NARRATOR: Bloom's Taxonomy Level: Synthesis -- combining ideas to create something new.
Mr. Carbis: The classroom has its own energy now. They're building things I never imagined.
Mr. Carbis: I'm not the fountain. I'm the architect. I design the experience.
NARRATOR: A PROJECT BOARD appears at the front of the room.
NARRATOR: Students now INFLUENCE each other through links. Use all your techniques!
NARRATOR: Guide them to create something greater than the sum of its parts.


[LEVEL 5 OUTRO]
NARRATOR: Mr. Carbis looks at the clock. An hour has passed without anyone noticing.
Mr. Carbis: I barely spoke today. And it was my best lesson yet.
NARRATOR: He thinks about Mrs. Okafor's words. Light the fire.
Mr. Carbis: The fire is lit. Now comes the hardest part.
Mr. Carbis: Letting go.


[LEVEL 6 INTRO — EVALUATE]
NARRATOR: LEVEL 6: EVALUATE
NARRATOR: Bloom's Taxonomy Level: Evaluation -- the highest order of thinking.
NARRATOR: Last day of term. Mr. Carbis stands at the door as students file in.
Mr. Carbis: Today, I want you to teach each other what you've learned.
NARRATOR: A PODIUM appears. Press E to invite students to PRESENT.
NARRATOR: Presenters boost everyone's attention, but need YOUR SUPPORT -- stay close and press SPACE!
NARRATOR: All techniques are available. Use everything you've learned. This is the final lesson.


[EPILOGUE]
NARRATOR: The bell rings for the last time this term.
NARRATOR: Students stream out, talking, laughing, still debating the day's presentations.
Student: Mr. Carbis? Thanks. For, like... actually making us think.
NARRATOR: He nods, unable to speak past the lump in his throat.
NARRATOR: The classroom empties. Chairs askew. Whiteboard covered in student handwriting.
NARRATOR: He sinks into his chair and looks at the ceiling.
Mr. Carbis: Did I make a difference?
NARRATOR: He thinks about the student who asked 'but why?' on day one.
NARRATOR: Today, that same student stood at the podium and explained WHY to the entire class.
Mr. Carbis: Yeah. Yeah, I think maybe I did.
NARRATOR: He packs his bag slowly. Turns off the lights.
NARRATOR: In the empty parking lot, under a sky turning gold, he unlocks his car.
